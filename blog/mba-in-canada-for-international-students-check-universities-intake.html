MBA is one of the top programs students can pursue to widen their spectrum of job opportunities. MBA provides specialization in diverse fields, which allows business aspirants to learn a wide range of skills.

Studying MBA from a top Canadian institute gives you an added advantage. Canada has some of the world's top universities, and the degree gained from these institutions are highly valued. It is why thousands of students go to study in Canada to pursue their favourite preferred and experience an unparalleled quality of life. This blog gives detailed information on studying an MBA in Canada, including the types of MBA - 1 year MBA programs in top Canadian institutes, documents required, intakes, scholarships offered and more.

Why pursue an MBA in Canada?

Canada is among the top destinations for pursuing higher education. Some leading institutions offer MBA programs after which job opportunities are immense. The country is a booming economy with high living standards and is known for offering affordable and quality education. Cities such as Vancouver, Toronto, Calgary, and Montreal are ranked at the top for the best places to live, and the part time work opportunities while you are studying in Canada help you support your finances and studies.

Benefits of studying MBA from Canada

•The process of admission for studying in Canada is an easy one.
•The institutions in Canada offer education that is highly recognized and is affordable.
•The internship programs at Canadian institutes helps you get an insight into the industry.
•An MBA degree helps you gain practical knowledge, develops leadership skills, and enables you to increase your earning potential.

Types of MBA in Canada

The institutes in Canada offer different types of MBA degrees to match the needs and requirements of domestic and international students, such as:
1) Full-Time MBA: Full time MBA program lasts one to two years, and students can study full-time. In this program, students can work with global industry leaders and learn from top instructors.
2) Part-time MBA: Part-time MBA is best for working professionals who want to pursue it while working simultaneously, though it takes longer to complete a part-time MBA than a Full Time.
3) Executive MBA: It is for those students who are planning to work while studying in Canada and are planning to gain practical and corporate knowledge along with classroom experience.

Which intake is the best for MBA in Canada?

Out of the three intakes in Canada, Fall, Winter and Summer, one of the most preferred intakes by international students is the fall intake. Almost every University offers courses in the fall intake and almost all the courses are available in this intake. Maximum number of students apply in this intake, and thus usually have a lower acceptance rate and the class size is also comparatively larger. During this intake, students have access to the majority of the scholarship option making it easier for students to bear the study abroad expenses.

Requirements to study MBA in Canada

Bachelor's degree: The candidate applying for MBA in Canada must have a bachelor's degree from a recognized university.
Work experience: Regarding MBA admissions, universities usually prefer candidates with work experience. It is not a mandatory requirement, but for some universities, it is. Try checking the requirements before applying will make it easier for you to make a decision.
English language proficiency:  Students planning to study MBA in Canada and from a non-native English-speaking country must prove their English proficiency by taking IELTS or TOEFL.
GMAT: To get admission into MBA in a top university/college, you must get a good GMAT score. A good GMAT score helps you get easy admission and allows you to apply for scholarships. Different institutions have different GMAT score requirements; it is better to check the score requirements beforehand and work accordingly to achieve your aimed score.

MBA in Canada: Application Process

The application process for studying MBA in Canada usually takes a lot of time, so it is better to start your application process 1 or 1.5 years before you begin your session.

Documents you need to start with your application process.

Documents Required:Following are some of the main documents required while applying to an institution for pursuing MBA.
Academic record: You need to submit all the documents of your graduate program, including your transcripts.
Standard admission tests: The scores for the GMAT test and English language proficiency tests such as TOEFL/IELTS are also to be submitted.
Financial documents and Bank statements
Work experience: Documents that show your work experience.
Letters of Recommendation: You must provide your (LOR) from the supervisors/managers of where you are working or have previously worked. Each university has its LOR requirements, so you must fulfil them to get admission to your favourite institute.
Admission Essay or Statement of Purpose: You must prepare a convincing SOP that helps the admissions committee evaluate your profile.

How to get MBA Scholarship in Canada?

To study MBA in Canada, International students can apply for many scholarships. Here is step by step guide to apply for a scholarship in Canada -
1) Understand the requirements
There is an eligibility requirement set for every scholarship and program, and you must check the eligibility and apply for the scholarship only then.
 2) Look for the available scholarships and make your choice
As an international student, you can apply for various scholarships with different funding prices. Try applying for as many scholarships as possible.
 3) Early application is necessary
Apply for the scholarship as early as possible because a late application makes it difficult to get the scholarship.
 4) Reference letter
A Reference letter should have a letterhead and it should be signed. Prefer getting it written by someone who knows you, it should not be your family or relatives.
5) Academic and creativity
Along with academic merit, some scholarships are also offered based on sportsmanship or other talents.

MBA in Canada without GMAT

GMAT score is required for admission in most Canadian universities and colleges to study MBA. But, it is possible to study MBA in Canada without GMAT by meeting the following requirements.
•GPA of 3.0 or above in bachelor’s
•Work experience of minimum 2 years after 4 years of bachelor’s degree and a minimum of 3 years of work experience after 3 years of bachelor’s degree
•2-3 letters of recommendation (LORs)
•Resume/CV

MBA in Canada Job Opportunities

One of the main benefits of pursuing an MBA program from Canada is that you attract many job opportunities. Employers worldwide prefer MBA graduates and remain ready to offer them managerial positions. After pursuing your MBA, you can work in various sectors, including:
•Finance and Accounting
•Consultancy
•Healthcare Management
•General Management
•IT services
•Education
•Legal jobs

Besides this, MBA graduates can also work for numerous departments of the Canadian government and help run the administration effectively. Moreover, management graduates can also work in technical, sales, marketing, product, and project management.

FAQ

How much does MBA in Canada cost?
Canada has some of the top institutions that provide MBA programs. The average MBA tuition fee in Canada is around $30,000 - $40,000, with a range of $16,000 - $90,000.
Can I work full-time while studying MBA in Canada?
International students studying MBA in Canada can work 20 hours per week and work full time during their semester breaks.
Is a job guaranteed after MBA in Canada?
There are a lot of job opportunities after MBA in Canada. The country has some of the biggest firms offering lucrative job opportunities to MBA graduates. With your MBA from a reputed institution and the right skill set, you can quickly get a job after pursuing your MBA.
Is it worth going to Canada for MBA?
Canada has some top ranked universities, and the MBA degrees gained from these institutions are valued worldwide. After pursuing an MBA from Canada, you can soon get a high-paying job. Also, the cost of studying MBA programs in Canada is lower than that of the US, hence it is worthwhile to study MBA in Canada.
Is Canadian MBA valid in India?
Yes, a Canadian MBA is acceptable in India. A foreign MBA can easily help you get a lucrative job in India.
