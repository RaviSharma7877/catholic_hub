Top Things NOT To Do While Preparing For IELTS Exam:

They should also take time to prepare a solution, and not answer a question if they don't understand it. Additionally, students should not be afraid to correct themselves if they know they have made a mistake, as it can have a negative impact.

While preparing for the IELTS exam, students do several things that IELTS instructors recommend to avoid them at any cost. There are numerous things that student don’t know they are doing wrong unconsciously like, rushing over things, and answering questions by fluke, and more. Choose best IELTS Coaching institutes like Canamprep, that provide you best coaching to score better. These practices can prove to be disastrous for your scores. If you want to know more about these harmful practices, read below:

DO NOT rush to answer the questions:

IELTS experts at Canamprep recommend that candidates should take a second or two to ponder upon the answer before speaking or writing. It will give them time to organize their thoughts so that they could answer better.

However, if you rush to answer a question, there are chances that the answer might get disorganized and becomes hard to follow. Also, remember not to take much time to prepare a solution, as there is nothing like a perfect answer.

DO NOT answer a question if you don’t understand it:

Some students answer the question without understanding it completely. It can work negatively for you. It is therefore advisable to ask the examiner to repeat the question or explain a particular word. It is entirely okay to ask for clarification or some explanation. However, do not ask for again and again during the exam.

DO NOT be afraid to correct yourself:

There are times when students know that they have made a mistake, but don’t correct themselves thinking that it might create a negative impact. If at any point you feel that you had made a mistake, you can always correct yourself. It will create a positive impact. It will show that you have made an error and now you can fix it.

DO NOT memorize answers:

Some students make a mistake of preparing scripted answers ahead of time. It is a big fall, as examiners can tell that the answers are scripted. This habit can affect your score adversely. Therefore, it is advisable never memorize your answer beforehand.

DO NOT use complicated words

Some students make a mistake of using big complicated words thinking that it will impress the examiner. However, this strategy can backfire badly. Every word has its context and degree. If the words are not used in proper context and don’t sound natural, it will put a terrible impression on the examiner. Therefore, it is advisable to avoid using big words in the speaking and writing modules of the IELTS exam.

Also read: IELTS Exam Fee

DO NOT say or write nothing to very little:

Many times, when students don’t know the answer to any question, they prefer maintaining complete silence. It is a very harmful practice. Silence in place of speaking or writing an answer will leave them blank. Also, blank answers are marked incorrect.

Therefore, it is advisable to speak and write in your speaking and writing modules, respectively. Always remember that the examiner needs something to evaluate your language skills.

FAQ

What are some things to avoid while preparing for the IELTS exam?
Some things to avoid while preparing for the IELTS exam include cramming, ignoring the test format, not practicing enough, relying on memorization, and not seeking help when needed.
Why is cramming not a good idea while preparing for the IELTS exam?
Cramming is not a good idea while preparing for the IELTS exam because it is a comprehensive test that requires regular practice over time to develop the necessary language skills and test-taking strategies.
Why is it important to understand the test format while preparing for the IELTS exam?
It is important to understand the test format while preparing for the IELTS exam because it helps you to become familiar with the types of questions and tasks that you will encounter on the test, which can help you to develop effective strategies for answering them.
Why is practice important while preparing for the IELTS exam?
Practice is important while preparing for the IELTS exam because it helps you to develop and refine your language skills and test-taking strategies, which can improve your performance on the test.
Why is relying on memorization not a good idea while preparing for the IELTS exam?
Relying on memorization is not a good idea while preparing for the IELTS exam because it is a test of language proficiency, not knowledge. The test assesses your ability to use English in real-life situations, so simply memorizing answers will not necessarily help you to perform well on the test.
