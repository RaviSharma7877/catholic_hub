Masters degree in Data Analytics is a postgraduate program that equips students with the essential theoretical and practical knowledge to analyse and decode complex data sets. Through a combination of coursework, projects, and research, they learn more about statistical modelling, data mining, machine learning, data visualization etc. The primary goal is to teach them to independently identify patterns and trends, devise predictive models, and make informed decisions based on data-driven insights.

Why pursue Masters in Data Analytics in Canada?

Nearly every single business requires and accordingly hires data analysts to evaluate significant data sets to help them understand the trends and patterns. This is supposed to help them make better business decisions that make more profit. As a result, data analysis is a highly profitable profession for the practitioner themselves. Graduates can pursue careers in a field of their liking, such as finance, healthcare, marketing, and technology.

Knowing the value of a degree in data analysis, why should one choose Canadian universities and colleges for the same? Mentioned below are some reasons:

•Canadian economy: First and foremost, the Canadian economy is incomparably strong. Its technology and finance sectors are growing steadily, opening numerous job opportunities for data analytics professionals who can help organizations make data-driven decisions.

•World-class education: The country is well known for its research and teaching facilities across all educational institutes, especially with respect to data analysis.

•Increasing opportunities for data analysts: Both public and private organisations have expressed a requirement for data analysts in Canada. Thereby assuring students of job opportunities post graduation.

•Dedicated research institutions: Countless educational institutions in Canada are dedicated to facilitating research projects and making advancements in the field of data analysis.

•Multicultural society: Canada welcomes aspirants from all over the globe, allowing students to converse and learn from people from different cultures.

•Post-graduation work opportunities: After completing their Masters degree in Canada, international students are qualified for post-graduation work for a specified period.

Admission Requirements for Studying Data Analytics in Canada

While the admission requirements for studying a Masters in Data Analytics in Canada vary based on the institution and program, there are some general admission requirements:

  • Bachelor's degree: All applicants require an undergraduate degree from a recognized institution, with a minimum grade point average (GPA) of 3.0 or its equivalent.

  • Relevant coursework: They must have studied relevant subjects such as computer science, mathematics, statistics etc. Certain programs might require the student to have credits in calculus, linear algebra, and statistics.

  • English language proficiency: International students must also demonstrate their English proficiency via IELTS or other standardized test scores. The minimum scores needed for acceptance vary based on the institution and the program.

  • Letters of recommendation (LOR): Applicants need letters of recommendation from academic or professional references who can vouch for their scholastic capability and potential.

  • Statement of purpose (SOP): Aspirants need to write an essay summarising their academic and career goals, their motivations for pursuing a postgraduate degree in Data Analytics and how the program will bring them closer to achieving their goals.  

  • Resume: A resume or CV recapping the student’s academic and work experience as well as any relevant skills or certifications is also required.

  • GRE or GMAT scores: In some cases, applicants might need to submit GRE or GMAT scores, although this may be optional for some colleges and universities.

Types of Scholarships Available

Students can apply for various scholarships while pursuing a Masters in Data Analysis in Canada. The universities offer some scholarships during the admission process, which can be merit or need-based. Some private Canadian organizations and the Canadian government also offer numerous scholarships for domestic and international students. Mentioned below are some bursaries to consider:

Job Opportunities

Students pursuing a Masters in Data Analytics in Canada have several options available to them post-graduation given the growing demand for data analytics in various Canadian industries. These include:

  • Data Analyst: As a data analyst, one analyses broad datasets to identify trends and provide insights that help the company make profitable decisions.
  • Data Scientist: A data scientist builds predictive models with the help of machine learning algorithms and advanced analytical techniques to pinpoint patterns in data.
  • Data Engineer: A data engineer collaborates with data analysts and scientists and maintains the system that processes and stores large datasets.
  • Data Visualization Specialist: A data visualization specialist creates visual data models using Tableau and Power BI tools for the benefit of stakeholders.
  • Business Analyst: A business analyst studies the inner workings of a business and finds areas for improvement.
  • Business Intelligence Analyst: A business intelligence analyst builds dashboards and provides reports that shed light on business performance.

These are just a few of the jobs that graduates with a Masters in Data Analytics can pursue in Canada. Overall, pursuing a Masters in Data Analytics is an excellent choice for those looking to enhance their analytical and problem-solving skills and pursue a rewarding career in this growing field. FAQ

Which Masters degree is best for data analytics?
Some of the top Masters degrees in Data Analytics include - Masters in Science in Business Intelligence and Analytics (Data Analytics Track), Masters in Analytics, Masters in Science in Analytics, MS in Analytics, MS Data Analytics, Masters in Professional Studies in Data Analytics and MS in Data Analytics and Policy.
Do I need GRE for a Masters in Data Analytics in Canada?
While GRE isn’t compulsory for all Canadian universities, some programs and institutes do ask for the scores. It can serve as an alternative for GMAT and strengthens one’s application.
Does a Masters in Data Analytics have coding?
Data analysts aren’t required to possess advanced knowledge of coding however they do need to know the fundamentals in order to process and visualize data.
What is taught in a Masters in Data Analytics?
The Data Analytics curriculum covers topics like Python or R programming, descriptive statistics, inferential statistics, analytical tools and techniques, data blending and extraction, hypothesis testing, regression analysis etc.
Which programming languages are required to study Data Science in Canada?
Students need to learn Java, Python, Perl, SQL, and C++ in order to study Data Science.
Which city is the best for Data Science in Canada?
Since most of the top companies have their head offices in Toronto, Montreal, and Vancouver in Canada students should strive to study and work there.
