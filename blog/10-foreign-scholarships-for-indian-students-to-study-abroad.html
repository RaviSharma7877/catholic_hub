Scholarships are a form of financial aid provided to students to help them cover their education costs and other expenses. These are awarded based on merit, need or a combination of both. They can come from various sources, such as the government, universities, private organizations, corporations, foundations and individuals. Some scholarships cover the full cost of tuition fee and living expenses. They are generally known as fully funded scholarships.

Fully Funded Scholarships for Indian Students to Study Abroad

Two of the best grants for Indian students to study abroad include Fulbright-Nehru Master’s Fellowships and Fulbright-Nehru Doctoral Research Fellowships. Additionally, there are several countries that offer fully funded scholarships for Indians in particular. These include the UK, USA, Canada, Australia, China, Japan and most of the EU member states.

Steps to Get a Scholarship for Indian Students

Obtaining a scholarship is easy if one goes about the process strategically. Mentioned below are some tips:

  • Research scholarships: Finding the perfect scholarship and applying for it is time-consuming. Thus, one should start researching scholarships in advance so as to find the best options that suit their academic profile and interests.
  • Check eligibility: It is necessary to check the eligibility criteria for each scholarship since it varies based on the country or university one is applying to.
  • Prepare for standardized tests: Applicants require standardized test scores, such as the IELTS or TOEFL for English language proficiency or the GRE, GMAT, or SAT as proof of academic proficiency. Start preparing for these as soon as possible in order to get a good score.
  • Contact universities: Check the official website of universities or contact them directly via email to learn more about the financial aid options they offer.
  • Apply early: More often than not, scholarship deadlines are well before the start of the academic term. It is vital to keep oneself up to date about the timeline.
  • Write a strong application: Use the application essay as an opportunity to highlight any scholastic and co-scholastic achievements, personal strengths and career goals.

How can students increase their chances of being awarded scholarships?

Students can apply for various scholarships whilst studying abroad to decrease their financial burden. Although the process is highly competitive there are certain strategies and methods they can use in order to significantly increase their chances of being selected to receive a grant. Mentioned below are some examples:

  • Prepare and Follow a Schedule: Numerous scholarships have early deadlines which need to be checked by the students themselves. Make sure all documents are ready to go once the portals open up.
  • Thorough Research: Students should make sure they have a list of every single scholarship they might be eligible for regardless of how well-known they are along with their requirements. They should apply to as many of them as they can to increase their chances of getting one. Smaller scholarships frequently have less competition, enhancing one’s chances of winning.
  • Multiple Drafts: One should aim to create multiple drafts and play around with the structure in addition to writing different introductions and conclusions to pick one that is best suited at the end.
  • Individuality and Expression: Students should use an anecdote that sets their essay apart from the competition. One must showcase their passion, be clear about their objectives and most importantly be themselves.
  • Word Limit: Every word counts. Ensure that the essay does not go over the limit and there aren’t any unnecessary tangents in it.
  • Grammar and Formatting: There is no room for error when it comes to spelling, grammar or the essay’s formatting. Proofread it multiple times and get second opinions.
  • Extracurricular Activities: Many scholarships favour students who are interested in their communities and are well-rounded. Join sports teams and engage in volunteer work.
  • LORs: Request school teachers for strong letters of recommendation and give them plenty of time to draft them.
  • Tailored Application: Change the application to reflect the objectives of each scholarship. To learn what the scholarship provider values, do some research about the organisation.

Commonwealth Scholarship and Fellowship
Commonwealth Scholarships are given to qualified individuals who have the potential to make a positive impact globally. Around 800 students are allowed annually to pursue postgraduate degrees with UK universities as per their ongoing commitment to the Commonwealth. Students from India, a Commonwealth country, who can’t afford to fund their studies on their own can apply for this scholarship.

Eligibility Criteria
  • Permanent citizen of Commonwealth Countries
  • Pursued their studies in English

Covered Expenses
  • Tuition fee

  • Living expenses

What percentage is necessary for an international scholarship?
Candidates must earn good grades in order to pursue higher education overseas after their matriculation. In order to qualify for scholarships, students must have a grade point average of at least 60% to 85% in 12th grade.
Which nation easily offers full scholarships to Indian students?
Students can easily get scholarships in the US, UK and Canada with good portfolios.
What IELTS score is needed to qualify for a scholarship?
A candidate should receive a band average of 6.5 or above. Any amount of points above that improves their prospects of being chosen.
