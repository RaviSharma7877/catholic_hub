Overcoming Language Barriers While Studying Abroad

  • 375 Reads
  • 3 min Read
  • By:CANAM Group
  • Updated On:Jul 11,2023 11:13 AM IST

Telegram Whatsapp Facebook Linked In Twitter


Thousands of students from different parts of the world aim to study at prestigious institutes present all over the world. The very thought of going to a different country for higher studies makes one feel excited as there is a lot to explore and learn in a foreign country.

The euphoria of getting the desired course or program for higher professional goals often delights the prospective students. Many people have opted for studying abroad who are now in impactful positions across different industries.

In the pursuit of getting foreign education, there are many obstacles and challenges that one comes across, such as writing an SOP (Statement of Purpose) and clearing a language proficiency test. After having surpassed all of these difficulties, one finally gets to take off to study abroad.

After having arrived in a foreign country, there are some challenges faced by students, the most prominent one being the language barrier. The majority of students who go to a different country face issues as their native language is not spoken there.

The already tough situation gets further aggravated if the country is a non-English speaking one. One can get further intimidated after hearing announcements in some other language at the airport. This can actually make one feel out of place.

Here are a few situations where one may feel the need to use the native language:

  • Buying something from a nearby market
  • Visiting new places and interacting with people
  • Making use of public transport for traveling
  • Getting something done at residence from a local vendor

The day-to-day activities can take a big hit if one is not comfortable communicating in the local language. Here are some tips that can help overcome the challenges posed by the language barriers:

Translation tools

There are many tools available online and on mobile app stores, which can help translate some language into another. With the help of such tools, one can easily get the required work done.

Even though these tools can help but one may not be able to enjoy the very essence of speaking in a local language.

Make friends

One of the best ways of conquering this obstacle is to make friends with people who know the language and practicing the language with them. In this way, one will be able to gel in well with the new culture and learn new things about them.

This is the easiest as well as the most convenient way of learning a new language and getting oneself familiar with the new surroundings and environment.

Dictionary

Keeping a dictionary with oneself can also help solve language problems. Even though there are many online translators available, there are many people who still prefer using a physical copy instead of the digital one.

One can learn new phrases and words by randomly going through the dictionary, helping one get a better grasp of the language.

Initiate conversations

One needs to learn conversation starters and start experimenting by interacting with people. This can open many doors of opportunities for learning new words and phrases. Planning way beforehand can help one better execute and explore the language better.

By practicing this for some time, one gets better at conversating with people, helping one make the most of ones time while studying abroad.

All of the steps given above can be of great help to people wanting to move out to a new country. No matter how difficult the situation or a language, if one is determined and focused, success is not far. Once the steps given above are followed with due diligence, one can easily get hold of the local language of the new country.

Many of the students, at Canam Consultants, have moved out to different countries where they have to speak a different language. The experts at Canam have always been supportive of students dreams and have been guiding them to pursue what they deem fit.
