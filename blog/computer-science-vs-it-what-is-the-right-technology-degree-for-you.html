Computer Science VS IT: Where lies the difference?

Computer science as a field is about developing and testing of software and software systems. It involves careful study of and working with mathematical models, computational theory, algorithms, data analysis and security etc. Computer Scientists deal with developing computational models that work behind all software systems. They work on data transfers and storage, security, encryption or coding of software systems.


IT, on the other hand, deals with the study, development, management, support and implementation of computers and information systems. It deals with both the software and hardware aspects of the information technology. IT professionals’ kind of makes sure whether home, government or an organization’s computers, systems, and networks are working fine or not.

Computer Science VS IT: Careers

Career in Computer Science or IT will take you along diverse directions, but still they have distinct paths. A job in computer science would mean working with programming languages or data to develop or improve software products and systems. A job in IT, on the other side, will mean working with computer operations, networks, security etc. which facilitates other people in the organization in working smoothly.

Computer Science VS IT: Salaries

Salaries for computer scientists and IT professionals vary as the roles and job demand varies. Generally, a computer scientist job requires specialized knowledge and a degree. Therefore, salary of a computer scientist earns a salary which is higher than an entry-level IT job as an IT job doesn’t usually require a formal degree.

Computer Science VS IT: Degree

Several colleges and universities offer a degree in computer science like computer engineering. Some computer science degrees also offer information technology specializations. But what will you study say in a computer science or an IT degree and which one should you choose?

A computer science degree will teach you the mathematical concepts behind computer systems and their programs. This degree will teach you both computer software and hardware systems, data structure and artificial intelligence basics and various programming languages. You might need to take up mathematics, statistics or various engineering courses.
An IT degree will teach you necessary systems about computer systems, networking essentials, application development, security practices etc.

Computer Science VS IT: Which degree should you choose?

Many principles or concepts that are taught in computer science can be applicable to several IT jobs. Computer science degree is a wide field and will prepare you for jobs across various areas of computer as well as information systems. However, if you would like to specialize in information technology or specializations, then an IT degree will be beneficial for you. Other degrees like computer engineering or data science might also be useful for.

However, there is no such thing like better or the right degree for you. Both these degrees and specializations overlap each other. Both types of degrees lead to different careers and different pay packages and have their own specializations and subfields. You can choose any of these degrees provided you are passionate about working with technology and understand what each of these degrees imply. You can be rest assured that whether it is a computer science or IT degree, you will be able to land up a job with high salary. This salary will increase with your age and experience you gain. The saying that more you learn, more you earn goes well with these fields.

Computer Science VS IT: Skills Required

The skills that an IT Professional or a Computer Scientist requires overlap several times. There might be some additional skills required depending upon the job, but following are the necessary skills needed to be developed for these roles:
•Analytical Thinking
•Communication Skills (Both Written and Oral)
•Troubleshooting
•Attention to Details
•Creativity
•Project Management etc.

Computer Science VS IT: Final Thoughts

Many people get confused with this debate on Computer Science vs IT degree because they fail to come up with a conclusion. A combination of a degree and expertise along with knowledge in computer science can be deadly when it comes to acquiring employment and earning a high salary.

As far as jobs in computer science vs IT areas, computer science has a slight edge over information technology. But, to make a choice between computer science vs IT degree, reaching out to a career counsellor or an expert might not be a bad idea.

FAQ

Which degree is better computer science or IT?
There is no such thing as “better”. Both degrees are good for you as long as you are passionate about technology.
Should I choose B. Tech in IT or Computer Science?
A B.Tech in CSE will be better for you as it will give you knowledge about IT as well. This will widen your career choices.
What is the difference between MIT and M.Tech in CSE?
An MIT usually focusses on practical aspects of computing and meeting needs of end users. An M.Tech in CSE, on the other hand, focusses on computer, computing sciences etc.
Is Computing or IT a good degree?
Yes, it is one of the most sought after degrees as there are innumerable job options in this field.
Which qualifications are best for technology?
A degree in computer science, computer engineering, software engineering, network engineering, IT etc. are best for the field of technology.
