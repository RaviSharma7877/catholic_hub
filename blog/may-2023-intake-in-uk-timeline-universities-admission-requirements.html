Among all the popular study-abroad destinations majority international students prefer to study in the UK. The country houses some of the highest-ranked academic institutes offering education using advanced pedagogical methods.

Academic institutes in the UK accept admissions throughout the year based on the course and admission requirements. The three intakes for admission are winter, summer and autumn, beginning in January, May and September, respectively. While most UK universities offer courses for the September intake, some also offer courses for the May intake.

May intake is generally suitable for candidates who missed applying for September or January intakes due to any reason. To know more about this intake, continue reading the blog:

May is the least popular intake among international students to study in UK because not many universities offer courses during this time. Students generally take spring break in May, which is why several universities avoid offering study options for this intake

How Many Intakes are there in UK?

Universities and colleges in the UK accept admissions for three intakes, i.e. January/February, September and April/May. Here is the list of acceptable intakes:

IntakesAdmission Months

 Being a less popular intake for admission, the application deadline depends on the type of course and university a student chooses to apply for. Therefore, it is important for students to start the application process on time. Below is the detailed timeline for May intake:

Student Visa Process for May (Summer) Intake 2024:

1. In order to apply for May intake, students need to start their planning a year in advance. So to apply for the May intake next year, students should begin searching for programs that match their profile and the different institutes that offer these programs between August and September.
2. After finalizing the course of their choice, students should prepare for course-specific requirements such as language proficiency tests or other standardized tests between September and October.
3. Apart from preparing and attempting course-specific tests, students also need to gather all the documents required for application, which they should finish by the end of November.
4. After selecting the course, gathering the necessary documents and attempting the required exams, students should begin the application process by correctly submitting all the details. This can be done between the months of November and February.
5. Every institute generally takes a month to respond to the application request of students. So the next step is to wait for their response between February and March.
6. After finally receiving the offer letter from the applied institute, the final step is to make a visa application and fly to the country to begin the May session.

How to Apply for May Intake in the UK?


The application process for the May intake is similar to other intakes apart from the dates of completing the application process. As the application date and intake deadlines vary from institute to institute, students are advised to keep a check on the university website and the courses offered by them.

Students can apply for this intake independently by visiting the university’s application portal or can take the help of an education consultant who can provide them with expert guidance from making an application to receiving an offer letter.

Documents Required for Admission in UK Universities:

Every institute requires a certain set of documents from international students before accepting their application. While the list of documents differs from institute to institute, some of the essential documents needed for admission are:
• Academic Mark sheets of previously attended courses
• English language proficiency scores (IELTS, TOEFL, GMAT, etc.)
• Statement of Purpose (SOP)
• Letter of Recommendation (LOR)
• Resume

Why choose May intake?

Many factors influence the selection of an intake. Although May is the least popular intake for application to UK universities and colleges, it is also the intake with a minimum competition giving students more chances of getting selected for the institute of their choice.

Conclusion:

Various factors influence students’ choice of intake, such as previous academic records, test scores, application start and deadline dates, and so on. Therefore, it is important to take the assistance of study abroad consultants who are experts in the field and guide students in making an error-free application.

FAQ

What is the May intake called in the UK?

The May intake is commonly known as the summer intake in the UK.

When is the correct time to apply for May intake in the UK?

Applying for a university in the UK is a complex process as it involves many formalities. Therefore, the general timeline to apply for May Intakes in the UK is between October and January, as the classes commence in June or August.

What is the application deadline for the May intake in the UK?

The deadline to apply for May intake varies from institute to institute and also depends on the seats available for the courses offered by such institutes. However, it is advised to students to apply between the months of December and February to avoid any delay.

Which is the best intake to apply for study in the UK?

The best intake to apply for study in top UK academic institutes is either January or September, as most of the universities are open for admissions in these intakes.

Which course is most demanding in the UK?

Popular areas of study in the UK are:
• Creative Arts
• Culinary Arts
• Engineering
• Life Sciences
• Education/Teaching
• Computer Science
• Performing Arts
• Life Sciences and Biosciences

What is the minimum CGPA required for MS in the UK?

Every university has different CGPA requirements. However, the minimum admission requirement in any university is 3.5+ on a scale of 4 or 9 on a scale of 10.

How much gap is acceptable in the UK?

A study gap of up to 2 years is acceptable if the student is applying for an undergraduate or diploma course in the UK, and a gap of up to 5 years is permitted in the case of postgraduate courses.
