Master of Business Administration (MBA) has always been a popular postgraduate degree option among Indian students looking to gain advanced skills and knowledge in business and management. Pursuing an MBA in Australia not only allows international students to gain high-quality education at affordable tuition fee but also gives them the experience of living in a multicultural environment. The blog highlights some of the details that students should be aware of while applying for education in Australia:

Reasons to Study MBA in Australia

Besides gaining quality education, pursuing an MBA in Australia offers numerous career benefits to international students. Some of the popular reasons to study in Australia are:

  • Variety of MBA Programs: From full-time and part-time to online and executive options, Australian business schools offer MBA programs in numerous fields.

  • Scholarship and Financial Aid options: To help reduce the financial burden of tuition fee and living expenses, the Australian education system offers various financial aid options to deserving international students through scholarships, grants and bursaries.
    Internship and Employment Opportunities: With strong connections with local and international businesses, many Australian universities help students apply for internships and job placements. Gaining work experience while studying enhances the resume and increases employability upon graduation.

  • Post-study Work Opportunities: After completing an MBA, international students become eligible for a Temporary Graduate visa, allowing them to work in Australia for a few years, depending on the duration of their course. This visa provides valuable work experience and may eventually lead to permanent residency.

  • Diverse and Multicultural Environment: Australia is home to students from various cultural backgrounds, providing a rich and diverse learning environment.

  • English-speaking Country: As an English-speaking nation, studying in Australia helps improve English language skills, which is an essential requirement in today's global business environment.

  • Favourable Climate and Lifestyle: The country is known for its beautiful landscapes, beaches, and outdoor activities, offering students the chance to experience a high standard of living and a friendly, welcoming atmosphere.

MBA Specializations in Australia

Australian business schools offer numerous specialisations in MBA. Some of the popular MBA specialisations available in Australia include:

Type of SpecialisationDetail
FinanceIt focuses on financial management, investment analysis, corporate finance, and financial markets. This prepares students for banking, investment management, or financial consulting careers.
Human Resources ManagementThis deals with strategic management of human resources, including talent acquisition, employee relations, and performance management. Graduates under this specialisation can pursue careers as human resources managers, talent acquisition specialists, or organisational development consultants.
MarketingThis equips students with the skills to develop and implement marketing strategies, manage brands, and analyse consumer behaviour. It opens the door to marketing management, brand management, or market research careers.
Supply Chain and Operations ManagementThis covers the management of supply chains, logistics, and operations, with a focus on improving efficiency, quality, and cost-effectiveness. Graduates pursuing this specialisation can work as supply chain managers, operations managers, or logistics analysts.
Information Technology ManagementThis specialisation focuses on the strategic management of information technology resources, including IT infrastructure, data management, and cybersecurity. Graduates can pursue careers as IT managers, chief information officers, or IT consultants.
Entrepreneurship and InnovationThis specialisation teaches students the skills needed to develop and launch new business ventures or drive innovation within existing organisations. It enables graduates to work as entrepreneurs, business development managers, or innovation consultants.
International BusinessIt focuses on the complexities of conducting business across borders, including international trade, cross-cultural management, and global strategy. Graduates studying this can pursue careers as international business managers, export managers, or global strategy consultants.

Project ManagementThis specialisation equips students with the skills to plan, execute, and manage complex projects across various industries. Graduates can work as project managers, program managers, or project consultants.

Eligibility Requirements for Admission to an MBA Program in Australia

Eligibility criteria for admission to MBA in Australia may vary slightly based on the requirements of the academic institute, however, there are some common criteria that most business schools expect applicants to meet, such as:

  • Bachelor's Degree: Applicants must be a holder of a bachelor's degree or equivalent from a recognised institution

  • GMAT/GRE Scores: Many business schools require applicants to submit GMAT (Graduate Management Admission Test) or GRE (Graduate Record Examinations) scores as part of the application process

  • English Language Proficiency: Applicants from non-English speaking countries must demonstrate English language proficiency through tests like IELTS, PTE, TOEFL, etc.

  • Letters of Recommendation: Most MBA programs require at least two letters of recommendation from professional or academic referees who can attest to the applicant’s abilities, character, and potential for success in an MBA program

  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) or Personal Essay: Applicants must submit a well-written statement of purpose or personal essay outlining their career goals, motivation for pursuing an MBA, and why they have chosen a particular institution

  • Work Experience: Most MBA programs in Australia require students to have at least two years of full-time work experience relevant to the field of management or chosen specialisation

  • Resume or CV: A detailed resume or CV highlighting your professional experience, academic achievements, and any relevant skills or accomplishments is typically required.

Financial Aid Options for MBA Course in Australia

There are many scholarships and financial aid opportunities for international students pursuing MBA in Australia. Following are the types of scholarships students can choose and apply for:

Australian Government Scholarships:

Australia AwardsThese scholarships are offered to students from developing countries for a full-time postgraduate study in Australia and cover tuition fee, living expenses, health insurance, and other costs.
Endeavour Leadership ProgramThis program provides financial support for high-achieving international students pursuing postgraduate studies in Australia.

University-based Scholarships:

Many Australian universities offer scholarships specifically for international students pursuing MBA programs. Some examples include:

Melbourne Business School ScholarshipsVarious scholarships are available for MBA students, covering partial or full tuition fee.
AGSM Global Reach ScholarshipThis scholarship is offered by the Australian Graduate School of Management (AGSM) at the University of New South Wales for international students and covers partial tuition fee.
Monash Business School ScholarshipsMonash University offers several scholarships for international MBA students, based on factors such as academic achievement and leadership potential.

Private Organisations and Foundations:
Students can also explore some private organisations and foundations that provide scholarships and financial aid for international students studying in Australia, such as the Rotary Foundation, the INSEAD Alumni Fund, and the Sir John Monash Foundation.

Home Country Scholarships:
Students may can also apply for scholarships offered by the Indian government or private organisations in India, such as the Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR) and the Tata Trusts.

Note: To maximise the chances of securing financial aid, it is recommended that students take the guidance of study abroad consultants to find the most suitable option.

An MBA degree from an Australian business school opens up a variety of job opportunities for international students across different industries and functional areas. Some of the popular job roles for MBA graduates in Australia are:

  • Management Consultant
  • Finance Manager
  • Marketing Manager
  • Business Development Manager
  • Human Resources Manager
  • Operations Manager
  • Project Manager
  • Product Manager
  • Supply Chain Manager
  • Entrepreneur

Conclusion
Pursuing an MBA in Australia as an Indian student is undoubtedly a rewarding experience, offering students the chance to experience high-quality education, a multicultural environment, and potential post-study work opportunities. However, applying for preferred programs in Australian universities can be a complex process. Hence, it is always recommended that students seek the guidance of expert study abroad consultants.

FAQ

Which is a popular MBA course in Australia?
While MBA in International Business is one of the most demanding specialisations in Australia, an MBA in Marketing, Finance, Human resource, Entrepreneurship, and Economics are other equally important MBA specialisations in Australia.
Is it possible to study MBA without GMAT in Australia?
Yes, there are a few Australian universities that accept MBA applications without the requirement of a GMAT score. Students can check it with their counsellor to gain further details about the admission process for such universities.
Which is the highest-paying job in Australia after MBA?
Some of the popular high-paying jobs in Australia are Chief Executive Officer, Operations Manager, Project Manager, General/Operations Manager, Senior Business Analyst and more.
What is the criteria to get a full scholarship in Australia?
Scholarships are granted to students either on a merit basis or a need basis. While there are no qualifying exams for scholarships in Australia, students are required to demonstrate their eligibility for the scholarship they chose to apply for.
What is the required CGPA for a scholarship in Australia?
Students should be a holder of at least a 3.0 CGPA and above for PG courses and an Average equivalent score of 90 or above for UG courses.
