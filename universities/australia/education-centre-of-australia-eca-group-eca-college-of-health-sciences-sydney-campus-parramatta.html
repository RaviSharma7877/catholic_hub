Education Centre of Australia (ECA) Group - ECA College of Health Sciences - Sydney Campus (Parramatta) Overview

About Education Centre of Australia (ECA)
Education Centre of Australia (ECA) was established in 2006 and is at the forefront of Australia’s higher education sector. We are renowned internationally for the quality of our teaching, academic expertise and research integrity.

ECA is a diverse educational group that supports students throughout their academic journey, through English language courses, undergraduate and postgraduate degrees, across the broad spectrum of disciplines.

At the completion of your course, ECA provides professional internships to move you forward in your career. Integrity, equality, and inclusion are at the heart of what they do. ECA strives to achieve academic excellence and assist students in a supportive and welcoming environment.

About ECA College of Health Sciences (CHC)
The ECA College of Health Sciences is a private higher education provider delivering high quality education programs that prepare students for enhanced professional practice and leadership in their field while engendering lifelong learning and strong ethics.

At CHS we care about your needs and aspirations. Our faculty and staff understand that education is the key to intellectual enrichment, acquisition of fresh perspectives and professional advancement. Our courses are uniquely designed to impart management and leadership knowledge related to healthcare. We aim to develop the next generation of health managers and leaders who can act as agents of change and respond to the needs of the community and wider stakeholders. Our courses are designed to be engaging, challenging, flexible and rewarding. Our faculty and staff are here to help you realise your aspirations and achieve your personal goals. I would like to invite you to take a tour of our College and the facilities and services we provide. It is important that you familiarise yourself with the requirements of your course and the support and services that are available to help you complete your course successfully.

The CHS Advantage

  • Affordability - CHS understands that tuition fees are important consideration when students choose their course and education provider. CHS provides competitive tuition fees with payment plans to suit student needs.
  • Flexibility - You can customise/tailor your timetable to fit your studies around other commitments. Please see CHS course profile page for further information. International students are required to study full time on campus
  • Experiential learning cycle - CHS’s courses follow the experiential learning cycle from Kolb to support effective learning. It executes all four stage of a student progress through a cycle of:
    • Having a relevant past experience
    • Observation of and reflection on that experience
    • The formation of abstract concepts (analysis) and conclusions
    • Used to test hypothesis in future situations, resulting in new experience
    • Student support - As a College within the ECA Group with a reputation for student support and services we will provide quality services and personalised support to all of our students. We appreciate the need to give our students every support they need to succeed.
    • In-depth learning and new insights - Learn and develop new insights from our leading academics and industry practitioners.
  • Acquire the graduate attributes:
    • Professional competence: Graduates will embody a professional disposition committed to excellence in practice and service delivery that ensures the provision of safe, effective and efficient care.
    • Critical thinking: Graduates will be professionally engaged and reflective critical thinkers who take a questioning approach and embrace lifelong learning to influence practice, policy and research to achieve clinical excellence and transform healthcare services.
    • Effective communication: Graduates will have the ability to communicate effectively and appropriately in a range of contexts using verbal, literacy, numeracy and information technology skills.
    • Accountability & Ethical Practice: Graduates will behave in accordance with the rules, standards and requirements for professional practice.
    • Interpersonal skills: Graduates will understand the need for collaborative practice and the capacity to work effectively in multidisciplinary teams.
    • Leadership: Graduates will understand the importance of good leadership in the safety and quality of healthcare and have the capacity to demonstrate effective leadership behaviours.
    • Intercultural sensitivity: Graduates will demonstrate a non- judgemental approach and value diversity.

Education Centre of Australia (ECA) Group - ECA College of Health Sciences - Sydney Campus (Parramatta) Admission Requirement

The following requirements must be fulfilled to get admitted to the ECA College of Health Sciences

  • International students are considered on a case-by-case basis. There can be some additional requirements, depending on the program you are applying to.
  • Students are required to submit their original transcripts from their last attended academic institutions. If they are not in English, then submit the translated copies of the same along with the originals.
  • Financial support statement (original bank statement and sponsor letter).
  • Passport identification page with machine-readable code visible
  • Applicants must be 18 years old at the time of the beginning of the course.
  • You need to submit your English language proficiency test scores if you are coming from a non-English speaking country.

Education Centre of Australia (ECA) Group - ECA College of Health Sciences - Sydney Campus (Parramatta) Students

We offer a broad range of dedicated student support services, including airport pickup, accommodation, counselling, and Overseas Student Health Cover (OSHC).

Student Card - Student Card is extremely important as a proof of identification that you are a CHS student. Student cards are also to be presented in other instances such as sitting for examinations, availing student support services offered by CHS. For new students, the Student Card is normally ready for collection from the Student Services during the first 2 weeks of the trimester. In case of lost or stolen card, you can apply for a replacement card in-person via the Student Services (fees may apply).

Careers, Employment and Course Advice Services - If you are not sure which course or program suits your needs and aspirations, please contact our course advisory service to arrange a free confidential consultation. Our advisors can provide you with information to help you decide about the study pathway that best suits you. In addition to course advice, CHS’s experienced Careers & Employment team can help you understand more about potential employment opportunities after graduating from the chosen program.

Accommodation Services - 2Stay Accommodation is a service that works with accommodation providers to meet the accommodation needs of students who are newly arrived in Australia. CHS students can access the service through contacting 2Stay Accommodation directly. Please visit the 2Stay  Accommodation website for more information. 2Stay Accommodation can also assist you with the following:

  • Arranging airport pick-ups and drop-offs (fees apply)
  • General advice about living in Australia

Student Counselling Services - CHS offers free counselling services to students via Converge International. Student Services at each campus also has expertise in assisting international students. International students seeking access to professional services should contact Student Services which will assist with putting students in touch with the right service provider.

Disability Services - CHS’s campuses are accessible to students with disabilities. The College provides reasonable adjustments for students with disabilities. Please contact the Student Counselling Services to arrange a confidential consultation to help us assess your needs and how best can CHS support your needs.

Student Mental Health & Wellbeing at CHS - CHS offers a free counselling service to students. It is available on campus for Sydney students and by telephone and email for Brisbane students. Brisbane students can also be referred to an independent counsellor. The counsellor has expertise in working with international students.
