Indian students dreaming of studying medicine overseas can consider MBBS in Australia an excellent choice. The country promises a comprehensive medical education experience through its globally ranked universities, advanced healthcare system, and an ample of research opportunities. The country has produced some of the top medical professionals, practitioners and doctors from various parts of the country.

Read the blog to explore the top colleges for MBBS, syllabus, fee, eligibility criteria, and the future scope for Indian students.

Why study MBBS in Australia?

  • The curriculum in Australian universities is regularly updated to reflect the current best practices and advances in medical science
  •  An Australian medical degree is globally recognised and respected, enabling graduates from Australian medical schools to practice medicine in other parts of the world, provided they meet the necessary licensing requirements
  • Majority of Australian universities emphasise research, offering students opportunities to contribute to cutting-edge medical discoveries
  •  Australian medical schools offer a practical experience beyond simulation labs, allowing students to work in local hospitals and gain essential clinical skills
  • Studying medicine in Australia provides students with the opportunity to work with a diverse patient population, enhancing their understanding and skills in providing culturally competent healthcare
  •  Australian universities offer a supportive environment for international students, providing resources such as language support, academic writing workshops, and counselling services

MBBS in Australia: Quick Facts

  • To study undergraduate MBBS programs, students are required to present high school qualifications equivalent to Australian Year 12 and performance in UMAT or ISAT tests
  • For graduate-entry programs, a bachelor's degree and a good score on the GAMSAT or MCAT is needed
  • Students must pass the University Clinical Aptitude Test (UCAT)
  • Non-English speakers will need to provide evidence of language proficiency through tests like IELTS or TOEFL
  • WHO/MCI recognises most medical institutes in Australia
  • Australia offers many job opportunities to students post the completion of their degree
  • MBBS programs in Australia include significant practical clinical training in hospitals and clinics
  • Medical degrees from Australian universities are recognised internationally. Graduates are eligible to register as doctors in many countries around the world
  • The AMC is the body responsible for ensuring that standards of education, assessment and training of medical professionals

MBBS in Australia for Indian Students

Every year, students from all over the world come to Australia to pursue their Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery (MBBS). In Australia, the MBBS programme is a thorough programme that combines theoretical instruction with hands-on laboratory sessions. An MBBS programme in Australia lasts 5-6 years and involves disciplines such as Medical Chemistry, Physiology, Microbiology, Patient Care, Surgery, Epidemiology, Medical Genetics, Oncology, Gynaecology, and other services are available.

The MBBS degree trains students to practise medicine and later specialise in their chosen subject. Students with an MBBS degree can work as General Practitioners, Resident Doctors, Visiting Doctors, Medical Consultants, Teachers, Researchers, and others. Many MBBS graduates prefer to further continue their studies by enrolling themselves in an MD programme.

MBBS in Australia: Program Eligibility

  • Applicants must be 17 years old to be eligible to study MBBS in Australia. Furthermore, if a student intends to return to India after completing the MBBS programme, they must pass the NEET exam.
    Students should also have-
  • Completed a recognised board's higher secondary education with Chemistry/ Biology/ Mathematics as subjects
  • ISAT (International Student Admissions Test) is needed for all overseas aspirants. If the student’s residency status changes during the course of the selection period, the student would need to sit for both ISAT and UCAT.
  •  Undergraduate Medicine and Health Sciences Admission Test (UMAT) or University Clinical Aptitude Test (UCAT) must be passed
  •  IELTS or TOEFL scores for English language competency
  •  For admission to MBBS programmes at some Australian universities, GAMSAT or MCAT scores are also required; please check specific university criteria before applying.

MBBS in Australia: Required Documents

To apply for an MBBS program in Australia, students need to provide the following documents:

•    Passport
•    Academic transcripts
•    Proof of english proficiency
•    Statement of Purpose (SOP)
•    Reference letters
•    CV/Resume
•    Proof of funds
•    Health insurance
•    Medical and character certificates
•    Portfolio or additional tests (such as UCAT, GAMSAT or MCAT)

FAQ

Which are the top colleges in Australia for MBBS for Indian students?
Some of the top-ranked universities to study medicine in Australia are the University of Sydney, Monash University, The University of Queensland, and The University of Western Australia. Each offers globally recognised programs and research opportunities.
What is the fee structure for studying MBBS in Australia?
The cost of studying medicine in Australia varies on the basis of the university and the chosen program. Generally, the tuition fee for international students range between AUD 250,000 to AUD 375,000 for the entire course. However, it is important to check the specific tuition fee along with other additional costs, such as living expenses and health insurance.
What is the eligibility criteria for Indian students to study MBBS in Australia?
Indian students are required to complete 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as primary subjects. Along with the academic requirements, students need to showcase their proficiency in the English language through tests like IELTS or TOEFL. Some universities also require scores from an aptitude test like UCAT, GAMSAT, or MCAT.
What is the scope for Indian students after completing MBBS from Australia?
After completing MBBS in Australia, Indian students can pursue specialisations, engage in research or academia, or practice medicine. To practice in Australia, they must complete an internship and register with the Australian Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA). Upon returning to India, students need to clear the Foreign Medical Graduate Examination (FMGE) to practice medicine.
