Scholarships in Canada for Indian Students

  • 801 Reads
  • 3 min Read
  • By:CANAM Group
  • Updated On:Apr 28,2023 05:30 PM IST

Canada is undoubtedly a popular study destination among international students. While it is a great career opportunity, many students hesitate to apply due to the high cost linked to it. Read the blog to learn about ways to overcome this stress, types of scholarships available for Indian students, and the steps to apply for scholarships in Canada.

Canada has emerged as a popular study abroad destination for international students, especially those from India. Factors like high-quality education, world-class universities, and welcoming culture make it a desired choice to pursue post-secondary education. Since applying to study in Canada can sometimes be difficult for some students, Canadian universities and colleges provide scholarships to help all such deserving candidates, irrespective of their financial background.

Apart from this, the Canadian government also offers financial assistance through grants, scholarships and bursaries. This blog highlights some of the top scholarships available in Canada for Indian students to help them realise their academic dreams without worrying about anything.

Types of Scholarships in Canada for Indian Students

Canada offers several types of scholarships to both domestic and international students catering to different levels of study, fields of study, and student backgrounds. Some common types of scholarships in Canada are:
Type of ScholarshipDetails
Merit-based scholarshipsThese are awarded to students on the basis of their academic achievements, leadership skills, or community involvement.
Need-based scholarshipsAs the name suggests, these are awarded to students based on their financial need. Students applying to these scholarships are often required to submit documentation proving their financial situation.
Athletic scholarshipsThese type of scholarships are awarded to students who excel in sports and demonstrate strong athletic abilities. Many universities in Canada have athletic programs that offer scholarships to talented athletes.
Subject-specific scholarshipsThese are available to students pursuing specific fields of study or research areas.
Regional scholarshipsSome scholarships are limited to students from specific regions or countries. For example, the Canadian Commonwealth Scholarship Program (CCSP) is available to students from Commonwealth countries, including India.
Government-funded scholarshipsThese are funded by the Canadian government or provincial governments to promote education and attract international students.
University/College-funded scholarshipsThese scholarships are offered by the Canadian universities and colleges to attract and support talented students.
Fellowships and grantsAwarded to graduate students, researchers, or faculty members for research projects or advanced study, these often cover tuition fees, living expenses, and research costs.
BursariesSimilar to scholarships bursaries are typically smaller in amount and are based on financial need.

Popular Scholarships in Canada for Indian Students

•Canadian Commonwealth Scholarship Program (CCSP)- The CCSP is a prestigious scholarship program offered to students from Commonwealth countries, including India. The scholarship covers tuition fee, living expenses, travel costs, and other allowances for students planning to apply for Master's and PhD programs in various disciplines at Canadian universities.

•Ontario Graduate Scholarship (OGS) Program- The OGS Program is open to Indian students pursuing graduate studies in Ontario, Canada. This merit-based scholarship is awarded to students with excellent academic records and covers two or three terms of study.

•University of British Columbia (UBC) International Leader of Tomorrow Award- This merit-based scholarship is available to Indian students pursuing undergraduate studies at UBC. It is awarded to exceptional students with leadership skills, community involvement, and academic excellence. The scholarship amount varies depending on the student's financial need and covers tuition fee, living expenses, and other costs.

•York University International Student Scholarship Program- York University offers many scholarships for international students, including Indian students, pursuing undergraduate studies. These are awarded to students on the basis of their academic excellence, leadership, and community involvement of a student.

•Lester B. Pearson International Scholarship Program- The University of Toronto offers this prestigious scholarship to international students, including Indians, who demonstrate exceptional academic achievement, leadership, and community involvement. The scholarship covers tuition, books, incidental fee, and residence support for four years.

•Humber College International Entrance Scholarships- Humber College offers scholarships to Indian students pursuing undergraduate programs at the college. The scholarship is awarded on the basis of the academic excellence, community involvement, and leadership skills of the student.

•Trudeau Foundation Scholarships- The Trudeau Foundation offers doctoral scholarships to international students, including Indian candidates, who demonstrate exceptional academic achievements and leadership in their respective fields.

How to Apply for a Scholarship in Canada?

Here are the steps to apply for scholarships in Canada:

•Research scholarship opportunities: Begin with searching for scholarships based on academic background, field of study, and personal circumstances.

•Review eligibility requirements: Carefully read the eligibility criteria for each scholarship, as these vary based on academic achievements, nationality, financial need, or field of study.

•Prepare required documents: Most scholarship applications require students to submit all the necessary documents, such as academic transcripts, LOR, IELTS scores, and proof of financial need (for need-based scholarships).

•Frame a compelling essay: A well-written personal statement or essay significantly impacts the chances of securing a scholarship. Therefore, crafting a thoughtful and engaging piece highlighting student’s achievements, goals, and motivation for pursuing higher education in Canada is important.

•Complete the scholarship application: Carefully fill out the application form while following the instructions and guidelines provided by the scholarship provider.

•Apply for university admission: In many cases, candidates receive an offer of admission from a Canadian university before they can be considered for a scholarship. Be sure to apply for the desired program within the specified deadlines and secure admission.

•Submit your scholarship application: After compiling all required documents and completing the application form, submit the scholarship application by the specified deadline.

•Await the results: Patiently wait for the results as the review process can take several weeks or even months.

•Accept the scholarship offer: If you are awarded a scholarship, be sure to accept the offer within the given timeframe and follow any additional instructions provided by the scholarship provider.

There are numerous scholarships available in Canada for Indian students, which students can search and apply with the help of their study abroad consultant. These not only ease the financial burden of the student but also recognise and reward the academic excellence and leadership qualities of the student.

FAQ

How difficult is it to get a scholarship in Canada?
It is quite challenging to get a scholarship in Canada as it is awarded to candidates with a strong profile, well-written statement of purpose (SOP), well-drafted letter of recommendation and participation in extracurricular.
How can a student get a full scholarship in Canada?
In order to get a full scholarship, students need to keep the following documents ready:
•Online application form (keep the application as impressive as possible)
•All academic transcripts (Make sure to score high)
•Certificates that prove your eligibility
•Well-written Statement of Intent
•Aptitude Test Scores (GMAT, SAT, GRE, etc.)
•Proof of English Proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL, etc.)
How much percentage is required for a scholarship in Canada?
The percentage required to apply for a scholarship in Canada depends on the type of scholarship a student chooses to apply for. Some scholarships require students to have at least 70% in class 12th, while others require at least 80%. Therefore, students are suggested to inquire about their eligibility for scholarships with their Canam counsellor before applying for one.
Can I apply for a scholarship based on my IELTS score?
Yes, students can avail of scholarships based on their IELTS band score. British Council awards the British Council IELTS Scholarship to help students get access to international education.
Can Indian students study in Canada free of cost?
There are no universities that offer free of cost education to Indian or Canadian students. However, students can apply for fully-funded scholarships.
Is there any age limit to apply for a scholarship in Canada?
There is no specific age limit to apply for Canadian scholarships, but students need to keep their eligibility in mind.
