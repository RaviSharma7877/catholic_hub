Master in Law (LLM) is a globally recognised postgraduate degree designed to broaden the legal knowledge of students. LLM (Master of Laws) in Australia is a popular choice among many international students planning to study legal education abroad. Offered by top Australian universities, the course lasts 1-2 years and provides students with practical knowledge of how the law works in different countries and sectors of the world.

Why Study LLM in Australia?

Here are some key reasons that make Australia a desired choice for pursuing an LLM degree:

High-quality education: Universities in Australia are highly recognised for their excellent academic standards, rigorous curriculum, and modern pedagogy.

Diverse range of specialisations: Law schools in Australia offer a wide variety of LLM program options, such as international law, intellectual property law, environmental law, taxation law, and corporate law, allowing students to focus on their specific interests and career goals.

Internship and practical experience: Australian law schools often have strong connections with legal institutions and organisations, enabling students to enrol for internships that offer practical experience during their studies.

Networking opportunities: Students get the chance to network with academics, legal professionals, and fellow students from around the world while studying in Australia. Such connections help them to build future collaborations that further help them at the time of searching for job opportunities.

Multicultural environment: The country offers international students a unique opportunity to learn and interact with people from diverse cultural backgrounds, thus helping them to foster a more inclusive and global perspective on legal issues.

Excellent quality of life: Australia consistently ranks high in terms of quality of life, safety, and healthcare, making it an attractive destination for students who want to enjoy a high standard of living while pursuing their education.

Scholarship opportunities: Many Australian universities and organisations offer scholarships and financial aid to international students, making it more affordable to pursue an LLM degree.

Career prospects: Pursuing an LLM from Australia certainly opens doors to various job opportunities worldwide. Such graduates have the experience and knowledge required to work in law firms, corporations, government agencies and more.

Post-study work opportunities: For international students, Australia offers a post-study work visa, which allows graduates to work in the country for a certain period after completing their studies. This gives students the opportunity to gain valuable work experience and possibly secure permanent employment in Australia.

Entry Requirements for LLM in Australia

•Bachelor's Degree in Law: International students planning to apply for LLM in Australia must have completed Bachelor of Laws (LLB) or an equivalent degree from a recognised university.

Academic transcripts: Students must submit official academic transcripts from their undergraduate studies.

•English language proficiency: Every foreign student is required to showcase their proficiency in English. They can prove such proficiency by giving linguistic tests like IELTS, TOEFL or PTE. However, students need to keep in mind that every institute has mandatory score requirements for such tests.

•CV and personal statement: Applicants may also need to submit a curriculum vitae (CV) and a personal statement outlining their interest in the LLM program.

Note: Every institute in Australia has individual requirements for LLM. Therefore, students must take the guidance from study abroad consultants to find the eligibility details.

Documents required for application:

•Passport
•Bachelor’s degree or equivalent
•Self attested mark sheets of class 10th and 12th or equivalent certificates
•Proof of English language proficiency
•Work experience (if required)
•Resume
•Letter of Recommendation
•Statement of Purpose
•Proof of funds

Student Visa for Australia

Student visa for Australia falls under the Subclass 500 and is comparatively easier to get than in other countries. Students become eligible for a study visa with a maximum validity of 5 years after enrolling in a full-time study course. The requirements include:

•Proof of identity
•Proof of funds
•Visa fee payment
•Recent passport size photographs
•English language proficiency scores
•Proof of enrollment in an academic institution in Australia (Electronic Confirmation of Enrolment (eCoE) certificate)
•Genuine Temporary Entrant (GTE) statement (proof of your intention to come to Australia only to study and not to settle down here)
•Certified documents of academic results
•Australian approved health insurance
•Criminal records, if any
•Additional requirements by university

Is it worth studying LLM in Australia?

Studying LLM in Australia not only gives an excellent opportunity to study at globally ranked institutes but also adds to the career prospects of the candidate.

Which is the cheapest LLM in Australia for international students?

Many universities offer an online mode of the course, which costs less in comparison to regular courses.

Do all Australian universities offer specialisations for LLM in Australia?

No, not every university offers specialisations in LLM. However, some offer disciplines that students can pursue in case they wish to get more insight
