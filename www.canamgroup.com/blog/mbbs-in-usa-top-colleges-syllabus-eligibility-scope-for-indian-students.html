Embarking on a journey to become a doctor is a significant decision to make, and choosing to pursue MBBS in the USA can certainly add to the adventure and challenges. With its globally recognised medical institutions, advanced educational infrastructure and rich research resources, the USA stands as a prominent destination for aspiring doctors from India.

Here is a comprehensive guide to understanding what the USA has to offer for MBBS aspirants.

Why Study MBBS in USA?

Pursuing an MBBS degree in the USA can offer many benefits to international students. Here are some reasons why students might choose to study MBBS in the USA:

•    Medical schools in the USA provide excellent opportunities for research and innovation to students
•    The medical curriculum in the USA includes significant practical and clinical training. Students get to work in different clinical settings, understand the healthcare system, interact with patients, and learn from experienced medical professionals
•    Students graduating from US medical schools can practice medicine in nearly any country after fulfilling the respective nation's licensing requirements
•    Post the completion of MBBS/MD, students can further opt for specialisation in their area of interest for more in-depth knowledge and skills in a specific area of medicine
•    It allows students to communicate with people from different cultural backgrounds, enhancing their communication skills and global perspective
•    Graduating from a medical school in the USA often opens up high-paying job opportunities in hospitals, research institutions, and academia worldwide

MBBS in USA: Eligibility Criteria

Students planning to apply for MBBS in USA must keep in mind the following requirements, including:

  •  Bachelor's degree, typically in a science field
  •  MCAT (Medical College Admission Test) test scores
  •  Proof of english proficiency
  •  Minimum age requirement

MBBS in USA: Acceptable Standardised Tests

To become eligible to apply for MBBS in USA, international students need to clear a few standardised tests, including program entrance exams & english proficiency test scores. Following are some of the tests required for MBBS in USA:

  • MCAT: Commonly known as MCAT, Medical College Admission Test, is the most preferred aptitude test for medical colleges. The top universities consider 511 or above as a good MCAT score, with a minimum of 117 in each section.
  • NEET: National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET) is an all-India medical entrance exam but is also accepted by a few top universities in USA. A NEET score above the 50th percentile is considered acceptable for students from the unreserved category.
  • TOEFL: While there are many globally acceptable English proficiency tests in USA, TOEFL iBT is majorly accepted English proficiency test score for international students. For MBBS programs in the US, a TOEFL score of 100 or above is generally expected.

Scope and Opportunities after MBBS

After completing an MBBS/MD in the USA, graduates have a wealth of opportunities. They can opt for residency programs in their chosen speciality, embark on research careers, or return to practice in India (after clearing the Foreign Medical Graduate Examination).

Some of the trending career options are:

  • Research Scientist
  • Physician, Internal Medicine
  • Neurologist
  • Pathologist
  • Family Physician
  • Psychiatrist
  • General Surgeon
  • Physician, ER
  • Cardiologist
  • Radiation Oncologist
  • Neurosurgeon

FAQ

What is the eligibility for Indian students to pursue MBBS in the USA?
To apply for an MBBS course in the USA, Indian students must first complete an undergraduate program, typically in science field, along with the MCAT (Medical College Admission Test), a standardised examination required by the US medical colleges. Proficiency in English is demonstrated by tests like TOEFL or IELTS, are also required.
What is the duration of MBBS in USA?
The length of MBBS program ranges from 4 to 6 years, based on the course type and length.
What does the syllabus for MBBS in the USA entail?
Medical education in the USA includes a 4-year undergraduate degree followed by a 4-year Doctor of Medicine (MD) program. The MD program consists of a pre-clinical stage (2 years) involving theoretical studies and a clinical stage (2 years) involving clinical rotations and hands-on patient care experience.

What is the fee structure for studying MBBS in the USA?
The overall cost of studying MBBS in USA, including tuition, room, and board, varies from institution to institution. However, the approximate cost can range from $60,000 to $70,000 per year.
What is the scope for Indian students after completing their MBBS in the USA?
After completing an MBBS/MD in the USA, graduates can opt for residency programs in their chosen speciality in the US, pursue research careers, or return to practice in India after clearing the necessary examinations. The USA's medical education opens doors to global opportunities and advanced research.
Are Indian students allowed to work while studying MBBS in the USA?
On a student visa, international students in the USA can work part-time on campus for up to 20 hours per week and full-time during holidays and vacation periods.
What is the return on investment after pursuing MBBS in USA?
MBBS in USA offers a huge return on investment to international students. Currently, the average mid-career salary of MBBS graduates in the USA is 223,000 USD (1.84 crores INR)
