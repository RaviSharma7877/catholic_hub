A Master’s degree in Finance (MFin) is a year-long (in some cases two years-long) postgraduate degree that prepares students for work in the financial industry. It equips students with an understanding of corporate finance, financial and risk management and investment analysis.

Students graduate from the program after applying theoretical knowledge to practical financial problems in the form of a thesis, field study or project. The MFin syllabus includes primary finance topics like derivatives, portfolio management, financial economics and risk management. Oftentimes, students are also given the opportunity to specialize via electives such as financial technology, sustainable finance, or private equity.

This blog delves into the experience of completing a Master’s in finance in Canada, including details on top universities, eligibility, scholarships and jobs. Keep reading to learn more.

Why study Master’s in Finance in Canada?

There are several reasons why a student should consider Canada to study finance. Aside from its dynamic educational system and multicultural study body, the country also has a robust commercial sector and is known for its growing economy. Students get to learn and network with leading financial experts as well as acquire valuable experience via apprenticeships and co-op. Canadian universities and business schools themselves are well known for their teaching and research faculties. Furthermore, their graduates are highly sought after in the global financial market and easily find employment in asset management, consulting, investment banking etc.

Eligibility criteria for Master’s in Finance in Canada

In order to apply for a Master's in Finance program in Canada, applicants must meet the following criteria:

  • Bachelor's degree: All applicants require an undergraduate degree in a correlated discipline like finance, accounting, economics or business.
  • English language proficiency: They must demonstrate their proficiency through TOEFL or IELTS, as the courses are usually taught in English.
  • Letters of recommendation: Students need to provide recommendation letters from educational or professional sources.
  • Statement of purpose or personal essay: They need to provide an essay summarising their career plans and motivations for pursuing a Master's in Finance.
  • Resume or CV: Students need to give details of their work and educational experience.
  • GMAT or GRE scores: Some programs might require students to take and submit GMAT and GRE scores.

Scholarships available for students

To make programs more feasible, students can apply for various scholarships whilst pursuing a Master's in Finance (MFin) in Canada. These scholarships can be offered by the universities themselves during the admission process or merit-based or need-based. The Canadian government and some private organizations also offer numerous scholarships for both domestic and international students. FAQ

What is the difference between an MFin and an MBA?
An MBA degree centers around business whereas MFin programs focus on finance. The former is offered in both full-time and part-time formats and the latter is a full-time commitment.
Is a Master’s in Finance better than an MBA?
It all depends on one’s career goals, skill set and aspirations. Those who wish to work in the financial industry should pursue an MFin whereas those who find work in the business industry should consider doing an MBA.
What subjects are covered in Master’s in Finance?
Some core subjects covered in an MFin are accounting, data analytics for finance, personal assessment and development, financial accounting and analysis, investments and corporate finance and valuation.
Can I do a Master’s in Finance without work experience?
No, work experience isn’t necessary in order to be eligible for an MFin however it does strengthen one’s application.
What is the difference between an MFin and an MSc in Finance?
There is no difference between both programs as they are synonymous with one another.
